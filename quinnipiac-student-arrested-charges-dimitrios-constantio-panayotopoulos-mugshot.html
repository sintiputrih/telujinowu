Dimitrios Constantio Panayotopoulos, a first-year student, was suspended by Quinnipiac University in a shocking turn of events.

He was arrested on October 31st about a suspected arson at the Mount Carmel Campus residence hall.

Concerns and queries over the extent of the fire’s damage and campus safety have been brought up by the tragedy.

According to Quinnipiac’s daily crime and fire records, the allegations originate from an event in Dana English Hall on Halloween.

The Hamden Police Department responded to a call about criminal mischief on the Mount Carmel Campus shortly before 2:30 p.m.

Surprisingly, Panayotopoulos was apprehended around 5 p.m., more than two hours after the initial police response.

The delay in apprehending the suspect raises concerns about the timing of events leading up to the incident.

However, it’s still unknown what exactly Panayotopoulos is suspected of lighting on fire, how severe it was, and how much damage it caused. 

Quinnipiac University community members and students are anxious and waiting for further updates due to the lack of available information.

Similarly, Quinnipiac’s associate vice president for public affairs, John Morgan, announced on November 8th that the student had been suspended awaiting a behaviour meeting.

Purposefully starting a fire or explosion that “recklessly” puts a building in danger is known as reckless burning.

This offence carries a five-year jail sentence and a $5,000 fine. Panayotopoulos is charged with reckless endangerment and criminal mischief in addition to the reckless burning accusation.

There is a maximum punishment of one year in jail and a maximum fine of two thousand dollars for this crime.

Nonetheless, Panayotopoulos was freed from detention after posting a $1,000 bond, and his next court appearance is set for November 16th at the Connecticut Superior Court in Meriden.
