Does Meghan Markle get royalties from Suits on Netflix?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, July 21, 2024

Meghan Markle, the Duchess of Sussex, is widely known for her role as Rachel Zane in the popular legal drama series, Suits.

Her portrayal of the character in the show has not only gained her immense popularity but also raised questions about whether she continues to receive royalties from the show, particularly after its availability on streaming platforms such as Netflix.

Meghan Markle's Role in Suits

Meghan Markle starred as Rachel Zane in the legal drama Suits from 2011 to 2018, appearing in over 100 episodes during the show's seven seasons.

Her impressive performances in the show propelled her to fame and established her as a prominent figure in the entertainment industry.

Meghan Markle's Salary

During her time on Suits, it is reported that Meghan Markle earned approximately £37,000 per episode, amounting to an annual salary of £333,000.

Apart from her work on Suits, she also had other film credits, contributing to her overall net worth, which was estimated to be around £5 million.

Receipt of Royalties

Despite Suits' availability on streaming platforms such as Netflix and Peacock, the actors and crew members of the show are not necessarily receiving substantial residual checks from its streaming success.

It has been revealed that some of the original Suits writers received modest amounts in residuals, indicating that the financial benefits from the show's resurgence on streaming platforms have not been evenly distributed among those involved in its production.

Also, according to sources, Meghan Markle continues to receive royalties from Suits due to her appearances in more than 100 episodes of the show.

This ongoing stream of royalties has reportedly contributed to her financial stability and was instrumental in the acquisition of her and Prince Harry’s Montecito home after their departure from the royal family.

Impact of Streaming Platforms

The resurgence of Suits on streaming platforms has led to heightened viewership, with the show accumulating an impressive 3 billion viewing minutes in one week between Netflix and Peacock.

But while the show's popularity has soared, the financial benefits seem to be primarily reaped by the streaming platforms themselves, as the original writers and crew members receive relatively modest sums in residuals.

Patrick J. Adams' Perspective

Patrick J. Adams, who played Meghan Markle's onscreen husband, Mike Ross, on Suits, acknowledges the show's newfound popularity and gives credit to Meghan for her role in its success.

His public acknowledgment of Meghan's contribution to the show's popularity further emphasizes the impact of her performance and the ongoing relevance of Suits, particularly on streaming platforms.

While Suits continues to be a widely acclaimed show, the distribution of its financial rewards, particularly in terms of royalties, appears to be a complex matter.

Meghan Markle, as a key figure in the show, is believed to receive royalties from its continued airing and streaming, contributing to her financial standing.

But it's evident that the financial landscape of the entertainment industry, particularly in relation to streaming platforms, presents challenges in ensuring equitable compensation for all involved in a show's production.
