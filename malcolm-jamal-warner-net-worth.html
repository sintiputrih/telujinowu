What is Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s Net Worth and Salary?

Malcolm-Jamal Warner, an American actor, director, and musician, has a net worth of $6 million. He is most famous for his role as Theodore Huxtable in “The Cosby Show” and as Malcolm McGee in “Malcolm & Eddie.”

Warner has also worked as a producer and director, contributing to several successful projects. He has also done voice acting for “The Magic School Bus.” Recently, he appeared in TV shows like “Reed Between the Lines” and “The Resident.”

Early Life and Career

Malcolm-Jamal Warner was born on August 18th, 1970 in Jersey City, New Jersey. He was raised by a single mother, and his name was inspired by Malcolm X and jazz musician Ahmad Jamal. Warner developed an interest in acting at a young age and attended various acting schools. He later became a child performer and attended The Professional Children’s School in New York City.

Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s Breakthrough Role and Successful Directing Career

In 1984, Malcolm-Jamal Warner landed the role of Theo Huxtable in “The Cosby Show,” which he played for six years until 1992. He was personally chosen for the part by Bill Cosby after auditioning. Warner’s success as a child actor paved the way for him to explore other areas of the entertainment industry, including directing.

Warner directed music videos for groups such as New Edition, Special Ed, and Five Star. He also directed several episodes of “The Cosby Show,” as well as sitcoms like “All That,” “Kenan & Kel,” and “Malcolm & Eddie.”

One of Warner’s most notable directing achievements was a short public health video about the dangers of HIV and AIDS for teens. The video was widely praised in 1992 for its informative and educational content.

Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s Career

After his tenure with “The Cosby Show,” Malcolm went on to book roles in shows like “Malcolm & Eddie.” He also worked heavily with “The Magic School Bus,” becoming a notable voice actor in the show. Another notable role during this period was in the show “Jeremiah.”

Throughout the rest of the 90s, he also appeared in shows like “Listen Up!,” “CBS Storybreak,” “Touched by an Angel,” and “The Fresh Prince of Bel Air.”

By the 2000s, Warner had shifted his attention partly towards music. He released a debut EP called “The Miles Long Mixtape,” and in 2007 he released another CD. During the 2000s, Malcolm also appeared in shows like “HawthorRNe” and “Community.”

In the 2010s, Warner continued to appear in shows like “Reed Between the Lines,” “American Crime Story,” and “Suits.” Towards the 2020s, Malcolm became known for his roles in shows such as “Major Crimes” and Amazon Prime’s “Sneaky Pete.”

In 2021, it was reported that Warner had acquired the film and television rights to the book “Freedom’s Shore” by Russell Duncan.

Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s Past Relationships and Current Family Privacy

Malcolm-Jamal Warner had a high-profile relationship with Michelle Thomas, who played his girlfriend on “The Cosby Show.” They dated until 1994, and Thomas passed away in 1998. Warner was reportedly with her at her deathbed. He then dated Karen Malina White for several years before dating Regina King until 2013. Although he is currently married with a child, he keeps his family’s identity private due to privacy concerns.

Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s Home in Studio City

Malcolm-Jamal Warner’s primary residence is a large hillside home in Studio City, California. He acquired the property in 1994 for $470,000.
