Fernando Torres’s Career

Club Career

Torres made his debut in 1998 when he won his first significant youth trophy, the Nike Cup while playing for the Athletico Madrid under-15 squad.

After earning a contract with Athletico the following year, he began his professional career.

Subsequently making a professional debut at the age of  16 with Atletico Madrid.

Further, The legendary striker Torres scored 82 goals in 214 league matches during his time with the club.

Later, he was then transferred to English club Liverpool for a price of $27 million.

Evidently, he was nominated for the FIFA World Player of the Year award in 2008; however, he finished third behind Cristiano Ronaldo and Lionel Messi.

Moreover, Torres scored his 50th goal for the club in the Premier League in 2009, making him one of the league’s fastest players to achieve the record.

To summarize his Liverpool career, he scored well over 65 goals in 102 appearances for them.

On 31 January 2011, he signed a five-and-a-half-year deal with Chelsea for an estimated to be $80 million.

Moreover, Torres played in the 2012 UEFA Championship League Final against Bayern Munich, in which Chelsea won 4-3 on penalties following a 1-1 tie.

To sum up his first full season with Chelsea, he scored 11 goals in 49 games.

Subsequently, the Spanish striker moved to the Italian Serie A team AC Milan on a two-year loan after a year.

Additionally, he agreed to join his former club, Atletico Madrid, on a season-long loan deal for the 2014/15 season.

During the later stage of his career, he signed for the J1 league team Sagan Tosu on July 10, 2018.

In addition, the legendary striker announced his retirement from all sorts of football matches on June 21, 2019.

International career

Fernando Torres made his international debut in the Euro under-16 cup in February 2001, and Spain won the European Under-16 Championship.

Furthermore, he also ended as the tournament’s best scorer and was awarded player of the tournament.

Likewise, Torres earned his first senior debut for Spain on September 6, 2003, in a friendly game against Portugal.

In fact, one of his most successful years was 2008, when Spain won the European Championship.

After winning the Euro 2008, Torres and Spain’s national team clinched their first-ever FIFA World Cup championship in 2010.
