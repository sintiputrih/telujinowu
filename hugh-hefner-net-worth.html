Hugh Hefner | Playboy Mansion, Cars & Private Jet

Playboy Mansion

Oh! The notorious Playboy mansion

Hugh lived in the famed Playboy mansion located in Los Angeles, California, from 1974 until he died in 2017.

The mansion became quite famous for the extravagant parties that Hugh would throw to film stars and media personalities, and socialites.

Contrary to popular belief, Hugh never actually owned the mansion.

Playboy Enterprises owned the mansion, and Hugh paid an annual fee to live in the mansion and throw the parties and everything in between.

The annual rent for the mansion came to around $1 million.

One year prior to Hugh’s death in 2016, Playboy Enterprises listed the mansion for sale for a staggering $200 million.

In the June of the same year, the mansion was sold to Daren Metropoulos for $110 million, but Hefner lived in the mansion till he died in 2017.

Hugh Hefner | Career

The founder of Playboy Enterprises, Hugh Hefner, was born on April 9, 1926, in Chicago, Illinois, United States.

He was the eldest child of Glenn Lucius Hefner, an accountant, and Grace Caroline Hefner, a teacher.

After high school graduation Hugh served in US Army from 1944 to 1946.

Similarly, he attended the University of Illinois and graduated in 1949.

After that, he began working as a copywriter for Esquire magazine in 1952.

Nonetheless, when asked for a $5 pay raise and denied, he quit his job.

The very next year, he took out a mortgage loan of $600 and raised an additional $8,000 to launch his own magazine with centerfold, Playboy.

The first issue of the magazine was published in 1953.
