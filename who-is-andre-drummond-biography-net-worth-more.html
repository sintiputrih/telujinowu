Who is Andre Drummond: Biography, Net Worth &amp; more

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, July 20, 2024

Andre Drummond is an American professional basketball player who plays as a center for the Chicago Bulls of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was selected in the first round of the 2012 NBA draft by the Detroit Pistons with the ninth overall pick. Drummond played one season collegiate basketball with the Connecticut Huskies before declaring for the draft. Over the years, he has achieved a number of milestones, including a two time NBA All-Star, All-NBA Third Team, four time NBA rebounding leader and NBA All-Rookie Second Team. Internationally, he was part of the United States national team that won gold in the 2014 FIBA Basketball World Cup.

Who is Andre Drummond?

Andre Jamal Drummond was born on August 10, 1993 in Mount Vernon, New York, United States to Jamaican parents. At a age 7, his mother moved to Middletown, Connecticut with Andre and his sister, Ariana Drummond.

He went to Woodrow Wilson Middle School for his early childhood education before starting high school at Capital Preparatory Magnet School in Hartford, where he played high school basketball.

As a freshman, Drummond averaged 12.7 points, 11.9 rebounds and 6.5 blocks per game and made better his performance in his sophomore year where he averaged 20.2 points, 16.6 rebounds, 7.2 blocks and 4.5 steals per game.

Drummond then took a transfer to St. Thomas More in Oakdale. Due to a stress fracture, he repeated sophomore year so to recover from the injury. There at St. Thomas More, he made a name as one of the most dominating centers in high school basketball.

Drummond enrolled at the University of Connecticut, despite initially declaring to spend a postgraduate year at Wilbraham & Monson Academy in 2011–12. After just one season with the Connecticut Huskies, he declared for NBA draft.

He was selected by the Detroit Pistons in the first round, ninth overall pick of the 2012 NBA draft. In his rookie season, Drummond averaged 7.9 points, 7.6 rebounds, 1.0 steal and 1.6 blocks in 20.7 minutes per game.

Drummond spent eight seasons with the Pistons before been traded to the Cleveland Cavaliers in exchange for Brandon Knight, John Henson and a 2023 second-round draft pick on February 6, 2020.

He has also played for the Los Angeles Lakers (2021), Philadelphia 76ers (2021-2022), Brooklyn Nets (2022) and currently with the Chicago Bulls since 2022 after he signed a two-year, $6.6 million contract.
