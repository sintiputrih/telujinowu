Billy Crudup’s major contributions also include his portrayal of the superhero Doctor Manhattan in Zack Snyder’s Watchmen. He played former US Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner in the 2011 television film Too Big to Fail, which was about the 2008 financial crisis.

Crudup also excelled in the Apple TV+ series The Morning Show, which earned him a Primetime Emmy Award in 2020.

Crudup was born in Manhasset, New York, amid the divorce and subsequent remarriage of his parents, Georgann (née Gaither) and Thomas Henry Crudup III. His father, who was described as an “incessant gambler and hustler salesman,” died in 2005, leaving behind a life filled with the constant pursuit of the jackpot.

Crudup may trace his family back to Congressman Josiah Crudup of North Carolina on his father’s side. William Cotter “Billy” Gaither Jr., his maternal grandfather, was a well-known trial lawyer in Florida, and his maternal grandmother later married Episcopal bishop James Duncan.

Crudup was the middle kid in a family of three brothers. His brothers, Tommy and Brooks, are both producers. His family moved from New York to Texas, and then to Florida, when he was about eight years old. He earned his high school diploma in 1986 from Saint Thomas Aquinas High School in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.
